Background
OOCA is a platform that connects users to certified psychiatrists and psychologists through video call.
The mobile application has been long due for a product restructuring, as well as a visual design redo. My role was to evaluate and redesign the existing product, keeping useful features, and creating new ones if needed with findings supported by user research. In addition, I consolidated the design direction, as well as the design system integrated with Figma. With these elements, I was able to smoothly introduce 2 new designers to the OOCA design ecosystem, and create a culture around design that was much more systematic.
Restructuring
Becoming mobile first
OOCA's users lean heavily towards the mobile platform, in comparison to the web version. Despite this statistic, ooca's mobile application lags in both technical capabilities and visual design. Our first step was to guide the development of the product to prioritize the mobile platform.
Design System
Functional and personal
ooca represents a more progressive and humanistic view towards mental health, moving away from stoic and formal interfaces of typical telemedicine interfaces.
However, as a start up on the smaller side, we understood that ooca's design system would have to last a long time before another retrofit. Therefore, the design system should be kept as functional and timeless as possible in order to be properly maintained in the future. This meant that it wouldn't follow visual trends that come and go seasonally. Our solution, therefore, incorporated elements within a simple UI structure that brough an air of friendliness to the platform: characters illustrations, plug and play banners, quotes.
Core features
Focusing on the appointment
The most rewarding experience feature for our users is the video call appointment with professionals, so we centered our core features around the call.
Onboarding
Supportive, inclusive, anonymous
Ooca's users are in South East Asia, where mental health care is still less recognized and accepted compared to the Western world. Therefore, we wanted ooca to be inclusive, supportive, and anonymous right from the start to make sure users felt comfortable on the platform.
Finding a provider
Topics of expertise
For both offline and online bookings, selecting a provider (inclusive of therapists, psychologists, and psychiatrists) is the biggest hurdle for users. Time with professionals costs a lot, and users are not guaranteed to find a good match from the start. To help users ease their experience and restructure the provider selection process, we asked our users what they looked for most when they selected a provider.
Through a series of user research interviews, we concluded that ‘Topics of expertise’ is what users looked for most. Our topics are curated to be diverse and specific. To help improve drop off rates, we decided to let users select their topics right after signing up, influencing recommended providers when they enter the application for the first time, and leading to their first booking.
Homepage
Through a series of prototypes and tests, we landed on a tab-based app architecture, each dedicated to an aspect important to our users. The flexible ‘banner’ changes to convey a mood, or provide interesting content. Below, a card-based UI, prioritizing a display of providers, allows to easily scale the dashboard as additional features get developed.
Provider card
Provider cards allow users to briefly view crucial information at a glance: topics of expertise, an image, and reviews.
New users
New users look for providers that are experts in their desired topics. To encourage both walkin and prior bookings, providers with selected topics of expertise and are online are first recommended.
Returning users
Returning users look for their previous provider, and consistently make bookings with the same 1 or 2 providers. To encourage follow up bookings, we included features that bring up a user's previous providers: favorite providers and visited providers. Cards of visited providers also show up on the homepage of returning users.
First impressions
Provider details
Provider details act as an online 'first impression' of a provider. Users book providers they feel connnected to, and prefer images over an informative list. We focused on displaying images, videos, and a quote before the fold to quickly deliver the message each provider conveys.
Booking
For features that were procedural like making bookings, we kept a simple flow to keep development efforts manageable. We were able to define the pain points from the older version of the app to streamline the booking process, while still providing a level of anonymity and support.
Video call
During the appointment
The ooca application provides the most value for users during the video appointment with providers. Therefore, we kept the interface as simple as possible, and focused our efforts towards the technical side to ensure that the video connection was always smooth. We also created a 'hide self camera' feature to help users better focus on the provider.
After the appointment
Reviews
We needed feedback from users to collect data, better our performace, and to provide more information for other users. Specific user reviews encourage other users to make bookings, so we included easily clickable tags that were low-effort and informative. These tags can be directly shown on user reviews. To gain insightful information, we also differentiated evaluations between ooca's service and the provider quality.
After the call
Visit note
One key feature we built in was the ‘visit notes’ that users receive 24-48 hours after their appointment. Users who visit ooca desire a way to reflect and remember, in the long term, what was discussed during their appointments. Visit notes keep track of the issues discussed, and advice from the provider. They allow the user to keep track of their progress within ooca, as well as solidify the rewarding experience even after the appointment.
